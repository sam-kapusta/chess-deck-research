Feigned Discovery Prompting — from C1 paper (arXiv 2603.20510)

The teacher LLM is instructed to reason as if discovering the solution, not justifying a known answer.

Key instruction: "Write natural chain-of-thought analysis arriving at {move}. Your analysis should cover... Never mention engine scores, ratings, themes, or that you were given the solution."

The teacher receives:
- FEN string
- Piece arrangement (explicit list of piece locations)
- Legal moves (all valid moves from current position)
- Opponent's last move
- Tactical themes (hidden hints, not shown to student)
- Master solution: Principal variation from Stockfish at depth 24

Generated reasoning includes:
- Board context: explicit square names ("queen on h5", "king on g1")
- Tactical relationships: attacks, pins, weak squares, defender counts
- Move justification: why the move works and opponent limitations
- Notation: UCI format with natural language clarification
- Length: 4-10 sentences scaled to puzzle complexity
- Style: objective voice without "I see" or engine score mentions

The key innovation is "feigned discovery" — the LLM reasons as if figuring it out, not explaining a known answer. This produces authentic-sounding reasoning that matches how a human coach would think through a position.

Removing this technique caused -24% accuracy degradation — the largest single ablation impact in the paper.

For generating training data:
1. Provide position + Stockfish eval + PV line to Claude Sonnet/Opus
2. Instruct it to reason through the position as if discovering the answer
3. The output becomes a training example for the student model (Qwen3-4B)
4. Binary reward during RL: does the model's final move match Stockfish?
